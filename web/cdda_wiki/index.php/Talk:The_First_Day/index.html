Very needed changes in philosophy, and a minor problem I spotted

So I just started CDDA a few days ago, and I tried using this tutorial, and this is my main criticism:

Throughout the tutorial actions are taken without explanations as to why, and only later are revealed as to their purpose. A balance must be kept between over-informing, and under-informing in a tutorial, while you do not need to go into the intricacies of the crafting system, and the philosophy behind the materials in the recipe, you do need to explain why, for example, I'm smashing a solar panel, beyond just, "It has something that's useful to you". Explain to me what the drops of the solar panel are, explain to me what I'm looking for, explain to me why I'm looking for those.

The tutorial could be easily improved by starting off with a checklist showing the things we need at the start, and then explaining how what we're doing relates to that checklist exactly. The bathroom part is a good example of what the tutorial should look like, it gives a list of the things we can find in the bathroom, it explains the uses of those different things, even if they aren't integral to the tutorial's path through the game, and then tells you how those uses align with our goals for survival; that is EXACTLY what this whole tutorial should look like.

Also in other news: The cudgel can no longer be expected as craftable by every variation of player within the given starting parameters, and a little maintenance could be useful.
